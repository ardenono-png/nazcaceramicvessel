---
title: "How did the role of polychrome vessels change over time in Nazca society? "
---

The significance of polychrome vessels evolved throughout Nazca history. During earlier periods, polychrome vessels were often indicators of social status. Highly decorated ceramics functioned as symbols of elite status, political authority, and religious ideology. Their decorative imagery helped communicate cultural and ceremonial meanings, making them important beyond their practical use.

By the later intermediate period, elaborate polychrome ceramics largely disappeared, as they were no longer regarded as important symbols of social status or a means to reinforce political authority. Instead, simply decorated ceramics prevailed, serving a more utilitarian purpose in daily life.
