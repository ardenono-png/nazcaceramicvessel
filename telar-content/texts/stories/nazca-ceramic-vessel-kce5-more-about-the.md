title: "More About the Spout!"
---


This flamingo vessel has a very unique spout: it is wider than that of most Nazca vessels, and appears to have a handle-like bar perfectly fitted for convenient carrying. Throughout the Early Intermediate period (100-650 CE), the Nazca people produced a wide variety of intricate ceramic forms, including bowls, bottles, dishes, and jars, with a wide variety of pigments and designs. At first, it may seem that this variety would make it more difficult to narrow down the function of such a unique spout. However, as previously stated, this vessel is conclusively fineware. While the vessel's spout is quite unique, its thinness eliminates all possibility of rigorous or intensive use. This is among several conclusions about function that can be drawn by making mindful observations of the vessel's features.
