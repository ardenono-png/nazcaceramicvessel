While creation of ceramics in Nazca society appears to have been at least semi-exclusive in practice and specialization, a more accessible, widespread practice of ceramics is not an impossibility. There are still clay sources existing across a wide geographic range in the area, and given social organization in Nazca society, distribution could have been facilitated by trade between specific communities. Given this vessel's intricate design, it very likely was made in a workshop within a concentrated area, by an artisan with a practiced skillset. At the same time however, it may have been stored or traded alongside vessels made by more casual and amateur potters.

This vessel featuring a motif of rodents may have shared some methods with the flamingo vessel: perhaps direct shaping for the base, and coiling for the upper body and cylindrical spout. Given the spout and handle of the flamingo vessel are more complex in shape, it would have likely required a higher variety of ceramic techniques than the rodent vessel.
