---
title: "Was this vessel used by elites or commoners?"
---


Although the production of polychrome ceramics was largely controlled by elites, polychrome vessels were found across multiple social groups in Nazca society. Certain vessel types, such as headjars, cup bowls, collared jars and modeled vessels were generally restricted to elites, with headjars and double-spout-and-bridge vessels being particularly prized, but more ordinary polychrome vessels were widely accessible across Nazca society. It is difficult to determine exactly who used this vessel, however, as a fineware vessel with elaborate flamingo imagery, it is quite likely it was used by elites. Curiously, polychrome vessels were distributed by elites to non-elites during feasting ceremonies to enhance and legitimize their political authority, which means vessels originally used by elites in ritualistic and ceremonial contexts could have ended up in the households of commoners via this distributional practice.
